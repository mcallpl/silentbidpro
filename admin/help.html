🚀 Step-by-Step: Setting Up Your First Auction

Step 1: Create an Event

Click "📅 Events" tab → "Create New Event" button. Fill in event name and dates.

Step 2: Add Auction Items

Go to "Items" tab → Click "+ Create Item". Add title, description, starting bid, and photo.

Step 3: Add Bidders

Go to "Bidders & Users" tab → Click "Create Bidder". Enter name and phone number.

Step 4: Launch Auction

Once items and bidders are ready, go back to your event and set the start time. Event goes live automatically.

Step 5: Monitor & Collect

Watch "Dashboard" for live metrics. Winners automatically get invoiced to pay via Stripe.

💳 Stripe Payment Setup

Overview

Stripe processes payments securely. Your nonprofit organization needs a Stripe account connected to Silent Bid Pro.

Setup Steps

  • Sign up for free at stripe.com (takes 5 min)
  • Verify your nonprofit status
  • Get your Stripe API keys (publishable & secret)
  • Paste keys into Silent Bid Pro admin settings

Multiple Events?

Each event can use a different Stripe account. Perfect for multi-org fundraising or separate campaigns.

💡 Pro Tip: Use Stripe test mode first to verify everything works before going live.

Security

Never share your secret API key. Payments are PCI-DSS compliant and 256-bit encrypted.

📦 Creating Auction Items

Item Essentials

Every item needs a compelling title, clear description, and realistic starting bid.

Required Fields

  • Item name/title (max 100 characters)
  • Starting bid amount
  • Minimum increment ($5 typical)
  • Close time (when bidding ends)

Optional But Important

  • High-quality image (first 200px shows in list)
  • Detailed description (see tips →)
  • Fair market value (helps bidders assess value)
  • Buy now price (optional early purchase option)
💡 Pro Tip: Items with photos get 30-40% more bids. Use clear, well-lit photos.

Organization

Group items into categories (Dining, Experiences, Services, etc.) to help bidders browse.

✍️ Writing Great Descriptions

The Formula

Hook (1 line) → Details (3-4 lines) → Value (1 line)

Make It Specific

❌ Bad: "Nice dinner for two"
✓ Good: "4-course dinner for 2 at Blue Hill with wine pairings and chef's tasting menu"

Include Details

  • When can this be used? (dates matter)
  • Who provides it? (vendor/business name)
  • What's included? (appetizers? drinks?)
  • Any restrictions? (weekends only? expiration?)
💡 Pro Tip: Use our AI description enhancer to automatically expand basic descriptions into compelling content.

Call to Action

End with a clear CTA: "A perfect date night. Bid now!" or "Limited availability - bid today!"

💰 Pricing Strategy & Reserve Prices

Starting Bid Strategy

Start at 40-60% of fair market value. Higher starts = fewer bids. Lower starts = higher final prices.

Conservative

Start at 40% FMV. Better for untested audiences. Builds momentum.

Aggressive

Start at 70% FMV. Works with experienced bidders who know value.

Increment Strategy

Item Value Min Increment Why
$0-$50 $2-5 Encourages competitive bidding
$50-$250 $5-10 Standard competitive range
$250+ $15-25 Avoids endless minor increases

Reserve Prices

Set a minimum you'll accept. If bids don't hit it, item doesn't sell. Builds credibility.

💡 Pro Tip: Items that sell at 130-150% of FMV are priced perfectly. That's the sweet spot.

❓ FAQ & Troubleshooting

Q: How do I increase final bid amounts?

Start at 50% of FMV, use minimum $5-10 increments, set competitive items at similar close times to create urgency, and use compelling descriptions with strong photos.

Q: What if someone doesn't pay after winning?

Silent Bid Pro sends automatic payment reminders. After 48 hours, the item goes to the next highest bidder. You can also manually re-open bidding.

Q: Can I run multiple silent auctions at once?

Yes. Create separate events. Each can have different items, bidders, close times, and even different Stripe accounts. Admins can manage multiple events.

Q: How much does it cost to use Silent Bid Pro?

SBB is free. You only pay Stripe's standard processing fees (2.9% + 30¢ per transaction), which are competitive with other payment processors.

Q: Can I have a reserve price on items?

Yes. Set a minimum acceptable price. If the winning bid doesn't meet it, you can choose not to sell the item or negotiate with the highest bidder.

Q: What payment methods do bidders have?

Credit/debit cards through Stripe (Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover). Payments are PCI-DSS compliant and 256-bit encrypted.
